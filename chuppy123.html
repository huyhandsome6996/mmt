1. Location, Terrain & Resources

  • Geographical Location:
    • North America and South America in the Western Hemisphere.
    • Bordered by: Arctic Ocean, Pacific Ocean, Atlantic Ocean.
    • Total area: ~42.13 million km².
    • Extreme points: Cape Murchison, Cape Freyark, Cape Saint Charles & Cape Branco, Cape Prince of Wales & Cape Parinas.
  • Terrain:
    • Western America: High mountains.
    • Eastern America: Lowlands and plateaus.
  • Mineral Resources:
    • North America: Copper, gold, lead, zinc, coal, oil, gas, Iron, bauxite, nickel,...
    • South America: Iron, copper, gold, silver, oil,...

3. Rivers, Lakes & Landscapes

  • Rivers and Lakes:
    • North America: Dense river network; home to the Great Lakes; main river is Mississippi–Missouri.
    • South America: Wettest continent; dominated by the Amazon River with the largest basin and highest water flow.
  • Natural Landscapes:
    • Very diverse (from cold to tropical).
    • North America: Forests, prairies, some deserts; includes giant Sequoia trees.
    • South America: Tropical rainforests (Amazon Rainforest), rich wildlife, and extremes like the Atacama Desert.

4. Population & Culture

  • Population:
    • ~1.06 billion people; uneven distribution.
    • Largest: United States, Brazil, Mexico.
    • Moderate growth, multi-racial.
  • Development Characteristics:
    • Major economic region, led by North America.
    • The United States dominates.
    • Large gap: rich North America vs. Poorer Latin America.
    • North → industry & services; South → raw materials.

5. Economy & Industries

  • North America:
    • Most developed region (global leader in tech & finance).
    • Agriculture: Highly modern, large-scale, very productive (few workers).
    • Industry: Advanced, shifting to high-tech (AI, aerospace, chips).
    • Services: Dominant (~75–80% GDP), especially finance & technology.
  • Latin America (South America & Caribbean):
    • Lower development, depends on resources & agriculture.
    • Agriculture: Major exporter (Brazil, Argentina).
    • Industry: Uneven, focused on mining & raw materials.
    • Services & trade: Export raw materials, import machinery; tourism important.
    • Key advantage: Panama Canal (global trade route).
